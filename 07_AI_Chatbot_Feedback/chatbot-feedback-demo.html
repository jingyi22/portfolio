Section 02

Trigger Timing Analysis

Feedback invitation timing is critical. We categorized trigger strategies into four models based on when and how users are asked for feedback.

Best Practice

BEST Model

Feedback collected 24–48 h after service completion. Allows reflection while experience is still fresh. Observed in Singapore Airlines — highest quality signal.

Good

GOOD Model

Feedback triggered after key milestones — booking confirmation, check-in, flight change. Contextual touchpoints without survey fatigue. Used by Expedia, Cathay Pacific, United.

Flexible

FLEX Model

User-initiated feedback available anytime through a persistent entry point. Lower immediate response rate but better for power users. Observed in British Airways.

Quick

QUICK Model

Feedback requested immediately after AI session ends. High volume but lower quality signals; risk of survey fatigue. Current approach at Trip.com and Agoda.

Section 03

Feedback Script Quality

The quality of feedback invitation text significantly impacts completion rates. We evaluated scripts across four quality tiers.

Full Script

Full Natural Language

Contextual, personalized invitation referencing the specific interaction. Creates perceived value. Highest completion rates observed — used by Singapore Airlines.

Simple

Simplified Prompt

Concise, action-oriented invitation with clear next steps. Good balance of completion rate and user effort. Used by Expedia, Cathay Pacific, United Airlines.

Non-Contextual

Generic / None

No AI-specific feedback or generic corporate phrasing unrelated to the chat session. Low engagement — signals weak investment in the AI improvement loop. Seen in Agoda, Amazon.

Optional

Opt-In Model

Feedback available through a persistent button that users must actively seek. Respects autonomy but captures only highly motivated respondents. Current Trip.com approach.

Section 05

Survey Content and Completion Methods

Two dominant patterns emerged for feedback format and survey delivery among platforms that have implemented AI feedback.

Inline Chat

Embedded Feedback

Feedback collected within the chat window via CSAT faces, thumbs, or star ratings. Minimal friction. Best for quick sentiment signals — highest completion rate observed across benchmarked platforms.

External Survey

Redirect Survey

Users sent to a dedicated survey page (Typeform, Qualtrics). Richer qualitative data but significantly higher drop-off. Recommended only for periodic deep-research studies.

Section 06

Optimization Recommendations

Three proposed options to improve Trip.com's AI chatbot feedback mechanism, addressing the current <1% evaluation rate.

Option A

Session-End Auto-Trigger

Automatically surface a feedback card at the end of every AI chat session. Simple thumbs-up/down with optional open text. Removes friction of finding the evaluate button; targets higher passive completion.

Option B

Milestone-Based Trigger

Trigger feedback only after high-value interactions — booking confirmation, cancellation, itinerary change. Ensures richer context per feedback point while minimising survey fatigue.

Recommended · Option C

Issue-Aware Feedback Flow

After AI session: invitation script → issue resolution check → CSAT (if resolved) or human transfer offer (if not) → factor selection → thank you. Separates resolved vs. unresolved cases for better signal quality. See interactive demo above.
